Favorite Things

If Oprah can have her list of "favorite things" then I suppose I can too. Here it is, a list of some tools, tricks and other resources I have found helpful.

Airtable

Airtable is a beautiful interface on top of a relational database. I recently began using it as a way to catalogue our house before we make the move to California. We had a complicated moving situation with the majority of our stuff going to storage and only a few things like clothes staying with us. In order to keep track of everything, I created a database of household items, and linked it to a table of boxes. Airtable made it super simple and flexible as we moved. I would highly recommend giving it a try.

Trello

I have been a pseudo-spokesperson for Trello ever since I was introduced to it back in 2013. Trello is a nifty "project management" tool, but its for much more than just projects. As an officer in the BYU student chapter of the Association for Information Systems, we used Trello to plan events, assign out tasks, and anything else we needed. Since being introduced to Trello, I have used it on numerous other projects and teams.
